# OGI Manifest

## Open Geopolitical Intelligence

### _Geopolitical intelligence for a better world._

---

## The Problem

When a conflict escalates, most people have access to the same thing: news headlines, opinion pieces, and social media takes.

What they don't have is the structured layer underneath — the timelines that show how a conflict actually developed, the actor assessments that explain what each side wants and why, the causal analysis that maps how one decision ripples into oil prices, political instability, and regional arms dynamics.

That kind of analysis exists. It lives inside governments, think tanks, and research institutions. It shapes real decisions.

It just isn't public.

---

## What OGI Does

OGI is an open-source, AI-powered platform for geopolitical intelligence.

For any conflict, it produces:

**Structured timelines.** Every major event, categorized, dated, and connected — from root causes to the present. Not a news feed. A navigable history of how a conflict actually unfolded.

**Actor assessments.** What each side wants, what their red lines are, what constraints they operate under, and what strategic logic drives their decisions — from their own perspective, not a single editorial view.

**Causal analysis.** How actions propagate through systems. How a sanctions decision moves through oil markets into inflation into political instability. How a proxy escalation connects to nuclear acceleration and reshapes a regional arms race. Made visible as an interactive graph.

**Systemic impact metrics.** Scored assessments of domestic stability, regional destabilization, economic shock, energy disruption, and alliance stress — each with confidence intervals and transparent reasoning.

**Policy simulations.** For any conflict: what would de-escalation actually require? What conditions would need to hold? What are the systemic trade-offs? Not prediction — structured exploration of realistic pathways.

Every analysis is versioned. When a new event occurs, a new snapshot is generated and stored. The full analytical history of a conflict is preserved and browsable.

---

## Why Open Source

A platform that makes claims about geopolitical actors and conflict dynamics cannot be trusted if it's a black box.

OGI is open source so that researchers can audit the methodology, journalists can verify how conclusions were reached, area specialists can correct errors, and anyone who disagrees can fork it and improve it.

The prompts are public. The data models are public. The analytical framework is public. There is no proprietary layer shaping what gets surfaced or how actors are framed.

This is the only architecture that can produce outputs worth trusting on a subject this sensitive.

---

## Who It's For

**Researchers and academics** who need a structured, citable analytical framework for conflict studies.

**Journalists** who want to go beyond the event and into the structural forces driving it.

**Policy analysts** who need to communicate systemic complexity clearly.

**Educators** teaching international relations, security studies, or political science.

**Engaged citizens** who want to understand the conflicts their governments are navigating on their behalf.

**Builders** who want to extend this infrastructure into domains we haven't yet covered.

---

## What Comes Next

The current version covers a single conflict. The roadmap:

- Live data ingestion from ACLED, UCDP, and open news sources
- Multiple simultaneous conflicts on the globe
- Inline source citations on every AI-generated claim
- Historical backtesting against resolved conflicts
- Public API for researchers and journalists
- Multi-actor negotiation modeling

The goal is not to build a product. It is to build infrastructure — a public layer of structured geopolitical intelligence that improves continuously as the community contributes.

---

## The Standard We Hold Ourselves To

OGI will never present a point probability where a range is honest. It will surface disagreement between sources rather than averaging it away. It will say "we don't know" when it doesn't know.

It will not advocate for any actor or policy position. It will present each side's strategic logic from their own perspective and make the reasoning behind every output visible and challengeable.

The only thing OGI cannot tell you is what is classified. And it will say so clearly when that's the limit.
