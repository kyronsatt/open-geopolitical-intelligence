# OGI Manifest

## Open Geopolitical Intelligence

### _Intelligence should be public._

---

## The Word They Kept From You

Intelligence.

Not in the trivial sense — not cleverness or IQ. The other kind. The kind that governments classify. The kind that shapes decisions about war, sanctions, alliances, and the fate of nations. The kind that tells you not just _what_ is happening, but _why_, _how_, and _what comes next_.

For most of human history, that kind of intelligence has belonged to a small class of people: analysts inside governments, advisors to the powerful, scholars behind institutional paywalls. Everyone else got the news — which is to say, they got the surface, stripped of context, stripped of causality, stripped of the structural logic that makes events intelligible.

This was never inevitable. It was a choice — made by accident, by inertia, and by the assumption that complexity requires credentials.

**OGI is built on the opposite assumption.**

---

## The Problem Is Structural

Every day, conflicts escalate, sanctions tighten, alliances fracture — and the public watches through a broken lens.

News is fragmented. Commentary is tribal. Government analysis is classified. Academic research is inaccessible. And the platforms that shape mass opinion — social media, cable news, partisan blogs — are structurally incentivized to inflame, not illuminate.

We live in an era of unprecedented geopolitical complexity. Great power competition, nuclear brinkmanship, proxy wars, economic warfare, cyber operations — the world is more entangled and more dangerous than at any point in living memory.

Yet the tools available to ordinary citizens, journalists, students, and even working policymakers remain primitive: opinion columns, Wikipedia timelines, and gut instinct.

The result is a civilization that makes collective decisions about war and peace — through elections, protests, political pressure, public opinion — with almost no structured understanding of the systems it is influencing.

This is not a knowledge problem. The information exists. The analysis exists.

It is an access problem. And access problems can be solved.

---

## The Vision

OGI — Open Geopolitical Intelligence — is an open-source, AI-powered intelligence platform for the public.

It is the first platform designed to take the kind of structured, multi-layered conflict analysis that has historically existed only inside classified systems and closed institutions — and make it fully open, fully transparent, and freely accessible to anyone on Earth.

OGI does not tell you what to think.

It gives you the same quality of structured reasoning that informs the people making the decisions — and lets you think for yourself.

---

## What Intelligence Actually Means

When an intelligence analyst assesses a conflict, they don't just read the news. They build a structured picture:

_Who are the actors? What do they want? What are their constraints? What are their red lines? What leverage does each side hold? What are the second and third-order consequences of each possible action? What does history tell us about similar configurations? Where is the uncertainty? What would have to change for the situation to shift?_

This is not magic. It is a methodology. A discipline. A way of organizing complexity into navigable structure.

OGI encodes that methodology — openly, challengeably, improvably — and runs it on every major conflict on Earth.

## What OGI Will Become

### A Living Map of Global Conflict

An interactive, real-time globe showing every active conflict on Earth — military, economic, diplomatic, cyber, proxy. Color-coded by intensity. Connected by lines of alliance, dependency, and hostility. Updated continuously as events unfold.

Not a news feed. A _systems view_ of the world.

### Structured Conflict Intelligence

For every conflict, OGI produces deep, layered analysis:

- Full escalation timelines from root causes to the present moment
- Live situational snapshots: military posture, economic pressure, diplomatic positioning
- Actor-by-actor strategic logic — _why_ each side believes what it believes, from their own perspective
- Systemic impact assessment: domestic stability, regional destabilization, energy markets, alliance stress
- Confidence intervals on every claim, with sourcing and reasoning made visible

Not opinion. Not narrative. _Structured intelligence, made public._

### Causal Reasoning at Scale

The hardest part of geopolitical analysis is not understanding what happened. It is understanding the chain of consequences that follows.

OGI models these causal ripple effects explicitly: how a sanctions decision propagates through oil markets into inflation into political instability across import-dependent nations. How a proxy escalation triggers a military response that accelerates a nuclear program that reshapes a regional arms race.

These chains exist whether or not they are modeled. OGI makes them visible.

### Policy Intelligence

For any conflict, OGI asks the question that intelligence analysts ask in closed rooms:

**What would it take for X outcome to occur, and what would it cost?**

Not prediction. Not advocacy. Structured exploration of the solution space — the conditions, trade-offs, and systemic consequences of each realistic pathway forward.

This is the question that should be asked publicly, before decisions are made — not reconstructed afterward.

### Multi-Actor Negotiation Modeling

In later versions: model actual negotiations. Represent each actor's constraints, red lines, domestic pressures, and strategic objectives. Surface the zones of possible agreement that exist even in apparently intractable conflicts.

Show that peace is not naive. Show that it is _structurally findable_, if the will exists to look.

### A Historical Intelligence Archive

Every conflict that ended left data behind. OGI will model historical conflicts retroactively — the Cuban Missile Crisis, the Suez Canal, the Korean War, the Iran nuclear deal — and test whether its models would have assessed them accurately.

This is how trust is built. Not by asserting accuracy. By demonstrating it against history.

### An Open Research Infrastructure

OGI will expose an API. Academic institutions will use it. Researchers will build on it. Datasets will be published openly. Methodologies will be peer-reviewed and publicly challenged.

This is not a product with a roadmap controlled by investors.

It is infrastructure — for a more rational, more informed civilization.

## The Design Philosophy

**Intelligence should be public.**
The core belief. Analysis of the quality that shapes decisions about war and peace should not be the exclusive property of governments and institutions. It belongs to everyone.

**Transparency over authority.**
Every claim cites its source. Every model exposes its logic. Every probability shows its range and the reasoning behind it. Users who distrust the output can trace it to its origin. There is no black box.

**Structure over narrative.**
The deepest enemy of clear geopolitical thinking is narrative capture — the story that makes one side heroes and the other villains. OGI replaces narrative with structure: actor objectives, constraints, incentives, trade-offs. No heroes. No villains. Only systems, and the humans operating within them.

**Calibration over confidence.**
Bad intelligence is confident intelligence. OGI will never present a point probability where a range is honest. It will surface disagreement between sources rather than averaging it away. It will say "we don't know" when it doesn't know — and explain why.

**Open over closed.**
The code is open. The data is open. The methodology is open. The prompts are open. Anyone can fork it, improve it, deploy it, challenge it. The only defensible neutrality is radical transparency.

## Who This Is For

**Citizens** whose governments make decisions about war on their behalf — and who deserve to understand what those decisions mean.

**Students and researchers** who need a structured, citable analytical framework that doesn't require institutional access.

**Journalists** who want to move beyond the daily news cycle into the structural forces driving events.

**Policymakers and advisors** who need a public-facing tool to communicate complexity without distortion or simplification.

**Educators** who want to teach geopolitics with analytical rigor, not ideological framing.

**Builders** who see this platform as infrastructure and want to extend it into domains we haven't yet imagined.

## Why Now

The technology is ready. Large language models can now process unstructured geopolitical text and produce structured analytical output at a quality that was impossible two years ago. Graph databases can model causal networks at scale. Real-time data pipelines from ACLED, UCDP, the World Bank, and open news sources make continuous ingestion feasible.

The need is urgent. The world is entering a period of compressed geopolitical crisis — faster than democratic publics can process it, faster than institutions can adapt, faster than the old frameworks can contain.

And there is a rare window right now: AI has captured public attention. People are asking what it can do for things that matter. We can show them an answer.

With the same quality of structured reasoning that has, until now, been reserved for the few.
